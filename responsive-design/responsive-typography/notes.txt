What it is:
- Responsive typography scales font sizes across viewports for readability.

Why it matters:
- Improves legibility and hierarchy across devices.

Key properties / syntax:
- clamp(), calc(), responsive units (vw, rem)

Interview questions:
1) Q: How to implement fluid typography?
   A: Use clamp() combining rem and vw values.
2) Q: Why not use vw alone for fonts?
   A: It may produce too-large or too-small fonts at extremes; clamp() constrains it.
3) Q: What is the base font-size strategy?
   A: Use rem-based scale for predictable sizing across components.
4) Q: How to test readability?
   A: Check line length, leading, and contrast across breakpoints.
5) Q: What metrics indicate readable typography?
   A: 45–75 characters per line is a common guideline.

Common gotchas:
- Using inconsistent base sizes causes scaling problems across components.

Pro tips:
- Define a type scale and express sizes relative to a single root variable.
