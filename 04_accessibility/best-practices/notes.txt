What it is:
- Practical accessibility rules and patterns to follow across projects.

Why it matters:
- Ensures inclusivity and legal compliance; improves UX for all users.

Key properties / syntax:
- Semantic HTML, labels, ARIA judiciously, visible focus, color contrast.

Interview questions:
1) Q: What is a skip link and why use it?
   A: A link to jump to main content for keyboard users to bypass repetitive navigation.
2) Q: What is focus order?
   A: Logical order in DOM for keyboard navigation.
3) Q: How to test accessibility?
   A: Automated tools + manual keyboard and screen reader tests.
4) Q: Why avoid relying on color alone?
   A: Color-blind users may not perceive differences; use text or icons too.
5) Q: What is accessible name computation?
   A: Algorithm used by AT to derive a name from labels, aria-label, or content.

Common gotchas:
- Neglecting mobile accessibility and touch target sizes.

Pro tips:
- Integrate accessibility checks into CI and design tokens for contrast.
