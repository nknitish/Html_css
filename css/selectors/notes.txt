What it is:
- Selectors target elements to apply styles: type, class, ID, attribute, combinators.

Why it matters:
- Precise selectors enable maintainable and efficient stylesheets.

Key properties / syntax:
- element, .class, #id, [attr=value], E > F, E + F, E ~ F

Interview questions:
1) Q: What is the difference between E F and E > F?
   A: E F selects descendants; E > F selects direct children only.
2) Q: What is an attribute selector?
   A: [attr=value] to match elements with attributes.
3) Q: When to avoid overly specific selectors?
   A: To keep CSS flexible and reusable; prefer class-based selectors.
4) Q: What is the general sibling selector?
   A: E ~ F selects siblings after E.
5) Q: How to select nth child?
   A: Use :nth-child(2n+1) etc.

Common gotchas:
- Using ID selectors for styling leads to high specificity; avoid for reusable components.

Pro tips:
- Favor class selectors and BEM-like naming for predictable specificity.
