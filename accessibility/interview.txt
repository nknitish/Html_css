Accessibility — Senior interview questions

1) How do you embed accessibility into the engineering lifecycle?
- Answer: Include a11y requirements in specs, automated checks in CI, component-level ARIA contracts, design tokens for contrast, and regular manual audits.

2) How to prioritize accessibility fixes in a legacy codebase with limited resources?
- Answer: Triage by user impact, start with keyboard and semantic fixes, add critical pages to a remediation backlog, and create patterns to prevent regressions.

3) How to measure accessibility beyond automated linting?
- Answer: Use manual screen reader testing, keyboard-only flows, user testing with diverse participants, and task-based success metrics.

4) How to design accessibility for complex interactive widgets (grids, charts)?
- Answer: Provide ARIA roles and keyboard schemas, maintain focusable controls, expose data via accessible tables or summaries, and document behavior for assistive tech.

5) How to make accessibility an organizational priority and avoid band-aid fixes?
- Answer: Train teams, incorporate a11y into PR review, reward accessible design, and provide easy-to-use component primitives that are accessible by default.
