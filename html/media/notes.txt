What it is:
- Media covers images, picture element, video, audio, and iframes.

Why it matters:
- Proper media handling affects performance, accessibility (alt text, captions) and UX.

Key properties / syntax:
- <img src alt>, <picture><source srcset>, <video controls><source>, <track kind="captions">, loading="lazy"

Interview questions:
1) Q: When to use <picture>?
   A: For art-direction or when serving different formats per viewport.
2) Q: What is srcset for?
   A: Provide resolution/size candidates for the browser to choose.
3) Q: How to provide captions for video?
   A: Use <track kind="captions">.
4) Q: When to use loading="lazy"?
   A: For offscreen images to improve initial load performance.
5) Q: What is alt text for?
   A: Describe image content for AT and in case image fails to load.

Common gotchas:
- Missing alt for informative images; not providing captions for video when needed.

Pro tips:
- Use modern formats (WebP/AVIF) with fallbacks; pre-generate responsive variants.
